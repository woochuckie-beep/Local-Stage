Why weekly care

A familiar face does something
a stranger never can.

Care isn’t a transaction — it’s a relationship. Our weekly model is designed for that. Here’s what changes when the same caregiver shows up, on the same day, every week.

Consistency

The same caregiver, same day, same time — predictable visits your loved one comes to count on without anxiety.

Relationship

Weeks turn into months. Their caregiver learns their stories, their preferences, the little things that make them smile.

Routine support

Meal prep, laundry, errands, medication reminders — built into a weekly rhythm that lifts the household.

Peace of mind

One person who notices when something is off. One coordinator who keeps your family informed. No scheduling chaos.

What happens without consistency

Every visit becomes a new introduction. A new person learning where the kettle is. A new face that, for someone with mild memory loss, feels like a stranger every time. It’s exhausting for them — and you’ll never get the relationship that’s the whole point of in-home care. Weekly is how we solve that.

Common questions

The questions families
most often ask us.

How much of this is covered by insurance or tax credits? +
More than most families realize. Our receipts qualify for the federal Medical Expense Tax Credit, which typically returns 15–25% of the eligible amount depending on your income. Most major Ontario extended-health plans (Sun Life, Manulife, Canada Life, Green Shield, etc.) cover a portion of non-medical home care — usually $1,500–$5,000 per year per insured. We generate the receipts automatically and email them monthly so your reimbursement is one-click. We’re not accountants, so we recommend confirming the specifics with your CPA or HR/benefits team, but in practice most families recover 20–40% of what they spend.
Why is weekly care your recommended option? +
Because the relationship between your loved one and their caregiver is where the value of in-home care actually lives. A familiar face on a predictable schedule reduces anxiety, builds trust, and lets us notice subtle changes over time. About 70% of families who start with a one-time visit move to a weekly plan within their first month — once they see the difference consistency makes, they don’t want to go back.
What if my caregiver isn't a fit? +
You meet your hand-matched caregiver before your first paid visit. If it’s not right at the meet-and-greet, we re-match before any charge. And every plan includes a 14-day fit guarantee: if the first two weeks don’t feel right, we re-match free or refund the unused days. We’d rather take the time up front than have anyone in your loved one’s home who isn’t right.
Can I pause my weekly plan? +
Yes. With 7 days’ notice you can pause your plan for travel, a hospital stay, or any reason at all. We hold your caregiver’s spot for up to 6 weeks. No fees, no negotiations — just send a note to your coordinator.
Can I change my plan if my parent's needs change? +
Of course — we expect needs to evolve. You can upgrade, downgrade, or add hours at any monthly billing cycle. If a sudden change is happening (recovery, decline, hospital discharge), your coordinator can adjust immediately and we’ll true up billing.
What if I need care this week — can you start fast? +
In most cases, yes. Same-week placement is possible when there’s a caregiver available in your area — usually for post-hospital discharge or sudden caregiver-relief situations. Call (905) 555-CARE and mention "urgent placement" and we’ll tell you immediately what’s possible.
Do you provide medical care? +
No. Hearthlane provides non-medical support: companionship, personal care, light housekeeping, meal prep, transportation, medication reminders. We work alongside your loved one’s nurses and doctors, but we’re not a substitute for clinical care.
What areas do you serve? +
We currently serve the Greater Toronto Area, including Oakville, Mississauga, Burlington, Hamilton, Halton, and central Toronto. Not sure if you’re in our area? Mention your postal code in the intake form and we’ll confirm right away.
Can I start with a one-time visit before committing to a plan? +
Yes — many families do. A single visit at $42/hr (3-hour minimum) is a great way to feel out whether Hearthlane is right for you. About 70% of one-time families end up moving to a weekly plan within their first month.
