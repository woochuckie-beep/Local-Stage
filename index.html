The real problem

Venues don't need to discover talent.
They need to not lose money.

What venues struggle with
❌
Empty rooms on slow nights
Booking an unknown act means gambling on turnout. A bad night costs bar sales, staff hours, and goodwill.
❌
No reliable way to vet emerging artists
Streaming numbers don't translate to local draw. A song with 50k plays might bring 8 people to a Tuesday show.
⚠️
Research takes hours they don't have
Scrolling Instagram, checking engagement, Googling show history — a booker already has a full-time job.
What artists struggle with
❌
Cold-emailing venues with no response
Most artist inquiries go to spam. Without a relationship or referral, there's no way in.
❌
No agent, no connections, no way in
Emerging artists have talent but no infrastructure or relationships to turn that into consistent, paid bookings.
⚠️
Existing platforms don't solve local
Bandcamp, SoundCloud, SubmitHub — none of them care about whether you can fill a room in Brampton on a Friday.
Why not just use existing options?

Ontario's scene already has networks.
Here's what's missing.

📱
Facebook & WhatsApp groups
Existing informal networks work on relationships and referrals. If you're new — as an artist or a venue — there's no door in. No data. No verification. Just hope someone vouches for you.
🎵
Bandcamp / SoundCloud / SubmitHub
Great for online discovery. Zero value for booking a Friday night show in Brampton. They don't care whether an artist can fill a room, can't verify live draw, and have no venue coordination layer.
🏢
Toronto booking agencies
Serve established acts. Not interested in emerging artists without a manager, press kit, or tour history. The emerging tier — with 200–3,000 local fans — falls through the cracks entirely.
LocalStage fills the gap between emerging artist and signed act — for Ontario's mid-market venues (50–300 cap) that want live music but can't access traditional booking infrastructure.

The only metric venues actually care about.

Streaming numbers lie. Follower counts lie. Even "viral" moments don't translate to bodies in a bar on a Thursday night in Mississauga.

LocalScore™ is built to answer one question: if this artist plays your venue, how many people will actually show up?

A note on our methodology LocalStage is early-stage. Some attendance data is self-reported by artists and cross-referenced with venue managers and public event pages — not pulled from a real-time API. We're transparent about this. Scores will become more precise as we build more verified show history. Artists whose data we can't verify are flagged accordingly.

Common questions

You probably have these.

How long does approval take?
+
We review every application personally, usually within 3–5 business days. You'll get an email either way — if approved, your profile goes live and we start pitching you to matched venues immediately. If we can't approve you yet, we'll tell you why and what to work on.
What happens if the artist cancels?
+
Artist cancellations are rare — every artist on our roster commits to strict reliability standards. If a cancellation does happen with 7+ days to go, we find a replacement within 48 hours at no extra cost. Under 7 days, the artist is liable for a documented penalty fee and you receive a full refund of the coordination fee.
How much do artists actually charge?
+
Artists set their own performance fees — LocalStage's $99 coordination fee is completely separate. Rates vary based on set length, draw, event type, and each artist's own assessment of their value. You'll agree on the artist's rate directly before anything is confirmed. We recommend confirming it in writing. If budget is a concern, let us know when booking and we'll match you with artists whose rates fit your range.
Can I contact an artist directly after booking?
+
Yes — once a booking is confirmed, we share the artist's contact details so you can coordinate logistics directly (load-in time, sound check, etc.). We stay in the loop. What you can't do is re-book an artist you met through LocalStage outside the platform for 12 months — that's in the platform agreement you sign electronically before your first booking. Bypass attempts result in immediate account suspension and the artist being notified. This policy is how we sustain the platform and keep artist fees fair for everyone.
I'm an artist. Do I need a manager or agent?
+
No. That's literally the point. LocalStage is built for emerging artists who don't have representation. If you have a genuine local following — even 200+ engaged fans — and you've performed live before, you're exactly who we're looking for. You apply, we review, and if you're a fit, we start pitching you immediately.
What if the venue ghosts or doesn't pay?
+
Every venue on the platform has been verified and must agree to our platform terms before booking. We document every booking in writing — venue name, artist, date, agreed fee — and both parties sign electronically. If a venue fails to pay an artist's agreed fee, we flag them immediately, suspend their account, and provide the artist with all documented evidence to pursue payment. LocalStage doesn't take a cut of your performance fee, so we have no incentive to side with the venue. We are building toward full escrow payment processing — and will roll it out as volume grows.
