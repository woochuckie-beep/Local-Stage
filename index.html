The real problem

Venues don't need to discover talent.
They need to not lose money.

What venues struggle with
❌
Empty rooms on slow nights
Booking an unknown act means gambling on turnout. A bad night costs bar sales, staff hours, and goodwill.
❌
No reliable way to vet emerging artists
Streaming numbers don't translate to local draw. A song with 50k plays might bring 8 people to a Tuesday show.
⚠️
Research takes hours they don't have
Scrolling Instagram, checking engagement, Googling show history — a booker already has a full-time job.
What artists struggle with
❌
Cold-emailing venues with no response
Most artist inquiries go to spam. Without a relationship or referral, there's no way in.
❌
No agent, no label, no leverage
Unsigned emerging artists have talent but no infrastructure to turn it into consistent bookings.
⚠️
Existing platforms don't solve local
Bandcamp, SoundCloud, SubmitHub — none of them care about whether you can fill a room in Brampton on a Friday.
How LocalStage works

Three steps from roster to revenue.

01
🎙️
We curate the roster
Every artist is vetted manually. We track local follower concentration, engagement rate, past show attendance, and reliability before adding anyone to the roster. No open-door policy — you have to earn your spot.
02
📊
We score real-world draw
Our LocalScore™ measures the one thing venues care about: will this artist bring people? We weight local audience, engagement quality, and proven show history — not vanity metrics.
03
🤝
We make the match
We pair the right artist with the right venue — genre fit, audience size, night of the week. Then we handle the intro, the coordination, and the follow-up so both sides can focus on the show.

Streaming numbers lie. Instagram follower counts lie. Even "viral" moments don't translate to bodies in a bar on a Thursday night in Mississauga.

LocalScore™ is built from the ground up to answer one question: if this artist plays your venue, how many people will show up?

We verify attendance numbers through venue managers and Facebook event RSVPs, weight followers by location, and factor in whether an artist has a track record of showing up prepared and on time.
